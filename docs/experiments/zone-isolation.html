Experiment 01

Zone1 / Zone2 Host Isolation

This page does two things: make the topology obvious, then make the results obvious. The goal is simple — instantly see who is in zone1 vs zone2 and why we ended up with zone1 → zone1 allow and cross-zone deny on TCP 18080.

Outcome
7 / 7
connectivity probes matched policy intent
Allow side — all zone1 internal probes on 18080 succeeded.
Deny side — zone1 ↔ zone2 in both directions were fully blocked.
Safety — only host service tested; no SSH / control-plane / Cilium datapath probes.

Topology

Three layers only: control plane, policy engine, managed estates. Jumpbox, placeholder nodes and raw YAML are omitted so the only thing you see is how policy flows from cluster-1 into zone1 and zone2.

Policy Design

ALLOW RULE
zone1 → zone1

For HostEndpoints with zone == "zone1", allow same-zone sources on TCP 18080.

DENY RULE
zone2 → zone1

Explicitly deny traffic from zone2 to any zone1 HostEndpoint on TCP 18080.

DENY RULE
zone1 → zone2

Explicitly deny traffic from zone1 to zone2 HostEndpoints on TCP 18080.

Conclusions

What this round proved

Calico can drive host-level zoning across cluster-2 nodes and standalone VMs without taking over the CNI on cluster-2.

Scope we deliberately limited

Only TCP 18080. No blanket zone firewall so we would not accidentally break SSH, the k3s control plane, or Cilium itself.

Next

Expand the current zone labels to multiple ports, richer profiles, and rules closer to real identity-based firewall models.
